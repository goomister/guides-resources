## Cannons
---
Cannons are the thing you fire on your Cat Base. Every once in a while (usually around 50 seconds, assuming you have a level 30 Cat Cannon Attack), your Cannon will be ready to fire. Some cannons fire by creating a “wave” which reaches up to 6,000 range, which is usually enough to cover most stages, while others will launch an attack in front of the nearest enemy. When you beat EoC 1, the Ototo Development Team is unlocked. Swiping to the right from GAMATOTO’s screen will allow you to interact with GAMATOTO’s brother, Ototo, who if given Engineers (gotten randomly from GAMATOTO Expeditions), Materials (given after beating Stories of Legend / Uncanny Legends stages), and real time can unlock the creation of new Cannons (but only after you first build the Cannon’s Foundation and Style). 

### Default Cannon
---
* Upgrade Priority: Not Applicable
* Stat Gains per Level: Not Applicable
* Usable At: Not Applicable
---

The Default Cannon should be your main Cannon until you unlock Breakerblast or get a level 5+ Thunderbolt. This Cannon is good for clearing weak peons on the field, resetting an enemy’s TBA, or for syncing enemies. Note that this attack counts as a Wave Attack, meaning Wave Immune enemies are not affected. The default Cannon isn’t bad, but compared to the other cannons, it gets outclassed really quickly. Its damage is super low (maxing out at around 3,600), so even during SoL you can start to notice the default Cannon dealing very low damage per hit. Once you unlock Breakerblast or Thunderbolt, your main lineups should use those Cannons instead of the Default Cannon. 

### Slow Beam
---
* Upgrade Priority: Low
* Stat Gains per Level
   * 1s at level 1
   * +2/3f per level from 2-10, then +5f per level from 11-30
   * 3.33s at level 20, 5s at level 30
* Usable At: Level 7/8
---
Slows all enemies with a beam that deals no damage and covers the whole screen, similar to the regular Cat Cannon, but without any delay. This Cannon, before maxed out, is extremely impractical to use because the Slow is way too short. The main applications you might use this Cannon for is to cheese stages by Slowing supporting enemies that appear on base hit, or to prevent Brollows from clipping into your units. However, once you enter endgame, the Slow Beam can be extremely powerful for rushes, given that being able to Slow enemies allows your units with pierce to force damage knockbacks onto them while also making sure they can’t move for a while. 

### Iron Wall
---
* Upgrade Priority: Low
* Stat Gains per Level:
    * 5 HP, 2s at level 1
    * +1 HP, +3/4f per level from 2-10, then +0/1 HP, +3/6f per level from 11-30.
 * 16 HP, 4.5s at level 20, 18 HP, 6s at level 30
* Usable At: Level 1
---
The Iron Wall spawns a metal Cat Unit in front of the nearest enemy. This counts as a Cat Unit, meaning that it will take up a slot in the Cat Limit, and is vulnerable to critical hits from enemies. This stays until it either dies or until its duration ends, whatever happens first. Like the Slow Beam, the Iron Wall is very impractical to use, although it is useful at level 1 compared to the Slow Beam. Because the Iron Wall is a Metal enemy and spawns instantly, it can be extremely helpful in certain occasions, like tanking Brollows on Pecking Order or making Metafilibuster restart his attack animation near the base on A Whole New World instead of moving towards your own base. For endgame strategies, the Iron Wall is also very useful for rush strategies for pinning enemies like Baron Seal. 
### Thunderbolt
---
* Upgrade Priority: Max
* Stat Gains per Level
 * 0.5s, 30% damage (compared to the normal Cat Cannon) at level 1
 * +1/2f, +1% damage per level from 2-10, then +3f, +0/1% damage per level from 11-30
 * 2s, 48% damage at level 20, 3s, 57% damage at level 30
 * +1% per level from 2-10, then +1/2% per level from 11-20, then +1% per level from 21- 30
* Usable At: Level 5
---
The Thunderbolt cannon will fire in front of the furthest ahead Cat unit on the field. This Cannon will Freeze those enemies for a set time and deal a little bit of damage. While at low levels this Cannon is pretty weak albeit pretty useful to give a bit of relief, when leveled up this Cannon is extremely broken given how strong applying Freeze to an enemy usually is. Thunderbolt also pairs extremely well with rushers like Bahamut, Yukimura, Fishman, Can Can, or Slime, allowing your units to get crazy value. This Cannon should be upgraded to 30 as soon as possible.  

### Waterblast
---
* Upgrade Priority: None
* Stat Gains per Level
 * 11% at level 1
 * +1% per level from 2-10, then +1/2% per level from 11-20, then +1% per level from 21- 30
 * 35% at level 20, 45% at level 30
* Usable At: Level 1
---
The Waterblast cannon will fire in front of the furthest ahead Cat unit on the field. This cannon deals a set amount of damage to Metal enemies which is equal to a percentage of their current health (think Metal Killer.) For any other enemies, the attack deals 0.1% of their current health rounded up, which is usually just 1 damage. Upgrading the Waterblast cannon does not affect its damage against non-Metals. Waterblast is “okay” at what it does, being able to chip a bit at a tough Metal enemy like Sir Metal Seal, but in any real stage this cannon is horrible, given that as the battle goes on Waterblast becomes more and more useless. It’s fine to use at level 1 for maybe a stage like Bionic Seaweed, but in any other case you should use a Cannon like Thunderbolt or Breakerblast. NEVER upgrade this Cannon as it is a waste of your resources. 
### Holy Blast
---
* Upgrade Priority: High
* Stat Gains per Level
 * 0.5% damage vs. Zombies, 10% damage vs. burrowed Zombies, 1s Freeze at level 1
 * +0.2/0.3% damage vs. Zombies, +0.5/0.6% damage vs. burrowed Zombies, +1/2f Freeze per level from 2-10
 * +0.7/0.8% damage vs. Zombies, +1.5% damage vs. burrowed Zombies, +1/2f Freeze per level from 11-20
 * +0.5% damage vs. Zombies, +1% damage vs. burrowed Zombies, +1/2f Freeze per level from 2-10,
 * 10% damage vs. Zombies, 30% damage vs. burrowed Zombies, 2s Freeze at level 20, 15% damage vs. Zombies, 45% damage vs. burrowed Zombies, 3s Freeze at level 30
* Usable At: Level 1
---
The Holy Blast fires a wave (which counts as a Wave Attack) across the battlefield, which only affects Zombies. The damage the Holy Blast deals is a percentage of their maximum health, which increases depending on whether or not the Zombie is currently burrowing. Alongside Freezing and dealing damage, it also has Knockback similar to the Default Cannon. Any Zombies that were burrowing will also be forced back to the surface, meaning for most Zombies they won’t be able to reburrow again. However, Zombies like Miss Haka or The Thrillerz with more than one burrow will be able to burrow again. The Holy Blast Cannon additionally has Zombie Killer and Soulstrike properties, which means that it can land the killing blow on a Zombie enemy. A Zombie’s corpse does not count as it burrowing. This Cannon is absolutely ridiculous against Zombies and will usually be your main way to beat hard Zombie stages like Prisoner’s Progress, Green Stone Desert IV or Heavenly Tower Floor 43. While it is specialized like the Waterblast Cannon, the sheer amount of abilities Holy Blast has is enough to make it powerful enough for you to level up to 30 immediately after you get a level 20 Thunderbolt. 
### Breakerblast
---
* Upgrade Priority: Medium
* Stat Gains per Level
 * 160 range, 30% damage (compared to the default Cannon) at level 1
 * +4/5 range, +2/3% damage per level from level 2-10
 * +12 range, +5% damage per level from 11-20
 * +8 range, +5% damage per level from 21-30
 * 320 range, 100% damage at level 20
 * 400 range, 150% damage at level 30
* Usable At: Level 1
---
The Breakerblast Cannon fires at the nearest enemy. When firing, this Cannon considers burrowing Zombies as being the “nearest” enemy, so be careful when using this Cannon in Zombie stages. When firing, Breakerblast delivers Knockback (which counts as an effect, meaning Knockback Immune enemies are safe against it), damage, and has a guaranteed chance to break an enemy’s Barrier. This Cannon is extremely good for regaining stage distance and pushing back enemies, working like the normal Cannon but being immediate rather than being on a delay, which does make quite the difference. The Barrier Break feature of this Cannon isn’t as notable (although it’ll help for Youcan or UltraBaaBaa stages), though if you have this Cannon you’ll be good for CotC 2 Aguham without needing to get Li’l Macho Legs or Loincloth. This Cannon doesn’t really need to be upgraded, but the range increase is a massive quality of life upgrade. 
### Curseblast
---
* Upgrade Priority: Low
* Stat Gains per Level
 * 1.1s at level 1
 * +3f per level from level 2-10
 * +6f per level from level 11-20
 * +6f per level from level 21-30
 * 4s at level 20, 6s at level 30
* Usable At: Level 1
---
The Curseblast is pretty similar to the Slow Beam, firing a “wave” across the field that applies a status effect. This time, it’s Curse, which nullifies effects like Weaken, Slow, Freeze, Knockback, Dodge, Curse, and Toxic. This Cannon is extremely niche and only really works for enemies that apply effects like Henry, Croakley, Mr. Mole, Queen B, or Heavenly Herald Papuu. This can be useful to get better rushdowns onto these enemies to kill them quickly, like against Papuu on Requiem for Angels using a normal strategy or on False Resurrection. This cannon doesn’t really need to be upgraded since the Curse duration will usually be shorter than an enemy’s attack cycle, but the extra uptime can be nice to have.
---
### Cannon Upgrade Priority
---
Build up to Breakerblast -> get a level 10 Cat Base Defense -> get a level 20 Thunderbolt -> get a level 20 Holy Blast -> get a level 30 Thunderbolt-> get a level 30 Holy Blast. The rest of the Cannons can be upgraded at your own leisure.
